# Business Health — Revenue & Growth Analysis
**Author:** Shivam Nagpal
**Date:** June 2026

---

## Headline

This business generated **₹33.38 crore in revenue across 3 months** 
(September–December 2025). Revenue peaked in October and has declined 
16% since. The decline is almost certainly seasonal — not structural — 
but the data window is too narrow to confirm that with confidence.

---

## Revenue Trajectory

| Month | Revenue | Orders | AOV |
|-------|---------|--------|-----|
| Sep 2025 | ₹97.2L | 1,065 | ₹9,124 |
| Oct 2025 | ₹11.56Cr | 13,148 | ₹8,791 |
| Nov 2025 | ₹11.10Cr | 12,586 | ₹8,823 |
| Dec 2025 | ₹9.75Cr | 11,157 | ₹8,735 |

September is an outlier — only partial month data, with 1,065 orders 
versus 12,000–13,000 in subsequent months. Excluding September, the 
trend is a steady decline from October through December. Order volumes 
fell 15% and revenue fell 16% over that two month period.

---

## AOV Trends

Average order value started at ₹9,124 in September and has declined 
to ₹8,735 by December — a drop of roughly 4%. This is not alarming. 

September's high AOV reflects early festive season behaviour — 
customers buying high-ticket electronics before the Diwali rush begins. 
As October arrives and mass-market festive shopping kicks in, cheaper 
categories like Beauty and Apparel drive volume up but pull the average 
basket size down slightly. This is healthy — more customers buying, 
not existing customers spending less.

To distinguish whether AOV rose because of pricier items or larger 
carts, we would compare average unit price versus average items per 
order over time. Here, the September spike aligns with electronics 
purchasing — high unit price, low quantity — suggesting it is item 
price driving AOV, not cart size.

## Category Performance

| Apparel | ₹3.33Cr | ₹2.79Cr | -16% |

| Home | ₹2.40Cr | ₹2.08Cr | -13% |

Apparel leads every single month — consistent across the entire 
data window. All four categories follow the same trajectory: peak 
in October, steady decline through December. No single category 
is bucking the trend in either direction.

---

## Are We Growing?

**No — but context matters.**

The business is not growing within this data window. Revenue has 
declined 16% from its October peak. However, this dataset covers 
only India's peak festive shopping season — Navratri, Diwali, and 
Christmas. A post-October decline is expected and normal for any 
Indian ecommerce business.

The more important question — is October 2025 larger than October 
2024? — cannot be answered with this data. Full-year data is the 
single most important thing needed before drawing any conclusion 
about whether this business is structurally growing or declining.

---

## What to Investigate Next

The even decline across all categories suggests a platform-wide 
seasonal effect rather than a category-specific problem. If one 
category had bucked the trend — grown while others declined — 
that would be the thread worth pulling. Instead, the story here 
is simple: festive season drove everything up, and the season ended.
